Mehrangarh fort, Johdpur, Rajasthan

History of Mehrangarh Fort, Jodhpur, Rajasthan:

Mehrangarh Fort is one of the most magnificent and iconic historical landmarks of Jodhpur, a city in the Indian state of Rajasthan. The fort's construction was initiated by Rao Jodha, the fifteenth-century Rajput ruler of the Marwar region, in 1459. The fort is located atop a 410 feet (125 meters) high hill called Bhaurcheeria (Bird's Hill) and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Legend has it that Rao Jodha founded the fort after consulting a hermit who resided on the hill. The hermit advised him to build the fort at that location to protect his kingdom from invaders. The construction of the fort took several centuries to complete, with different rulers adding various structures and palaces within its walls.

The fort's name "Mehrangarh" is believed to have been derived from two words: "Mihir" meaning Sun and "Garh" meaning Fort, which together signify "Fort of the Sun." This name is attributed to the Sun deity, which the Rathore rulers worshiped. Over the centuries, Mehrangarh Fort witnessed numerous battles and sieges, as it served as a strategic stronghold for the Marwar rulers. However, it remained unconquered by any external forces. The walls of the fort bear the marks of cannonball hits, testifying to the fort's defensive strength. Today, Mehrangarh Fort stands as an architectural marvel and a symbol of Rajput valor and pride. It houses a museum that showcases an impressive collection of artifacts, arms, armor, paintings, and intricate royal palanquins, providing visitors with a glimpse into Rajasthan's rich cultural heritage.
