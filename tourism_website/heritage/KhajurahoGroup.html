Khajuraho Group of Monuments, Madhya Pradesh

History of Khajuraho Group of Monuments, Madhya Pradesh:

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is a stunning collection of ancient temples located in the Chhatarpur district of Madhya Pradesh, India. These temples are renowned for their exquisite and intricate architectural details, including intricate carvings depicting various aspects of life, culture, and spirituality.

Origins: The construction of the Khajuraho temples is believed to have started during the reign of the Chandela dynasty, which ruled the region between the 10th and 12th centuries. The exact history and purpose behind the construction of these temples remain a subject of scholarly debate.

Chandela Dynasty: The Chandela rulers, who were known for their patronage of art and architecture, are credited with the construction of these temples. It is believed that over 85 temples were originally built, spread across an area of approximately 20 square kilometers. However, today, only around 20 temples have survived the ravages of time and are well-preserved.

Architectural Marvel: The temples of Khajuraho showcase a blend of Nagara-style and Dravidian-style architecture. They are predominantly made of sandstone, and the complex carvings on the walls depict scenes from mythology, celestial beings, erotic sculptures, musicians, dancers, animals, and daily life during the medieval period.

Rediscovery: Over time, the Khajuraho temples were gradually abandoned and became engulfed by vegetation and forests. They remained hidden from the world until they were rediscovered by a British surveyor named T.S. Burt in the mid-19th century.

UNESCO World Heritage Site: The Khajuraho Group of Monuments was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986, recognizing its cultural and historical significance. The site attracts tourists from all over the world who come to marvel at the intricate craftsmanship and artistry of these ancient temples.

Today, the Khajuraho temples stand as a testament to India's rich cultural heritage and architectural brilliance, drawing visitors and researchers alike to witness the splendor of this unique site.

Tourist Place Timing:

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is open to tourists throughout the year, and the visiting hours are generally as follows:
Opening Hours: 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM
The temples have specific entry fees for Indian and foreign tourists, and there may be additional charges for photography and videography. It is recommended to visit the Khajuraho temples during the early morning or late afternoon to avoid the midday heat and to enjoy the stunning play of light and shadows on the intricate carvings. Please note that the visiting timings may be subject to change due to unforeseen circumstances or special events, so it's always advisable to check the official website or local authorities before planning your visit to the Khajuraho Group of Monuments.
