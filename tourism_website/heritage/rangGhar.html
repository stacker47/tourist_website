Rang Ghar, Assam

History of Rang Ghar, Assam:

Rang Ghar is an iconic historical monument located in Sivasagar (formerly known as Sibsagar), in the Indian state of Assam. It is one of the most significant architectural landmarks in Assam and holds a special place in the state's cultural and historical heritage.

Origins: Rang Ghar was built during the reign of Ahom King Pramatta Singha in the 18th century. The term Rang Ghar translates to House of Entertainment or Amusement Pavilion. It was constructed primarily for the purpose of hosting cultural and recreational events, including traditional Assamese games and sports like buffalo fights, which were popular during the Ahom dynasty.

Architectural: The Rang Ghar is a two-story, oval-shaped structure, which is quite distinctive in its architectural style. The monument is constructed using bricks and indigenous materials, showcasing the artistic and architectural skills of the Ahom dynasty. The double terraces with arched windows and the sloping roof are some of its prominent architectural features.

Cultural Significance: Rang Ghar played a significant role in the cultural and social life of the Ahom dynasty. It was the royal pavilion where the Ahom kings and nobility would gather to witness various forms of entertainment, including Bihu dances, cultural performances, and sporting events. It was not only a center for amusement but also a symbol of the grandeur and opulence of the Ahom kings.

Tourist Place Timing:

The Rang Ghar in Sivasagar, Assam, is open to tourists during specific hours. As of my last knowledge update in September 2021, the general visiting hours were from morning to evening. However, visiting hours and access may be subject to change, so it's advisable to check with local authorities or tour guides for the most up-to-date information on visiting timings and any entry fees.
When planning your visit to Rang Ghar, it's also worth exploring the other historical and cultural attractions in Sivasagar, including the Ahom-era temples like the Sivasagar Sivadol and Joysagar Tank, which are significant to the region's heritage. Enjoying the local Assamese cuisine and cultural experiences can also make your visit to Sivasagar a memorable one.
