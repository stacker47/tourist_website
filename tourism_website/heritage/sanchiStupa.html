Sanchi Stupa, Madhya Pradesh

History of Sanchi Stupa, Madhya Pradesh:

Sanchi Stupa is an ancient Buddhist monument located in Sanchi town, Madhya Pradesh, India. It is one of the oldest and most well-preserved stupas in the country, representing an important site of Buddhist heritage and art.

Construction: The construction of the Red Fort was commissioned by the fifth Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1638. The fort was built as the new royal residence and served as the main seat of the Mughal power for nearly 200 years. The construction of the fort took around nine years, and it was completed in 1648.

Architectural Splendor: The Red Fort is an architectural marvel, showcasing a perfect blend of Persian, Timurid, and Indian architectural styles. The fort is made of red sandstone, giving it the distinctive red color, and its impressive walls extend to around 2.5 kilometers in length.

Layout and Design: The Red Fort is a massive structure covering an area of about 254.67 acres. It is enclosed by high defensive walls and features several grand gateways, including the famous Lahori Gate and Delhi Gate. The fort's interior houses several magnificent buildings, including the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), Rang Mahal (Palace of Colors), and the Mumtaz Mahal (Palace of the Chief Queen).

Historical Significance: The Red Fort witnessed several significant historical events, including the peacock throne's display, the proclamation of the Mughal emperors, and India's independence celebrations. The fort served as a symbol of the Mughal power and grandeur during their reign.

Post-Independence: After India gained independence in 1947, the Red Fort became the site of India's annual Independence Day celebrations. The Prime Minister of India hoists the national flag and delivers a speech from the ramparts of the fort on August 15th, marking the country's independence from British rule.

Today, the Red Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of India's most popular tourist attractions, attracting visitors from all around the world to witness its architectural beauty and historical significance.
